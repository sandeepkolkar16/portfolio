2025 · Research Project

Investigation of Mechanical Properties of Natural Fibre & Epoxy Composites

A research project studying the mechanical performance of basalt fibre reinforced epoxy composites as a sustainable alternative to synthetic materials.

  • Investigated composite materials reinforced with basalt fibre — chosen for its excellent mechanical properties, high thermal stability, and superior chemical resistance
  • Fabricated basalt fibre / epoxy resin specimens and tested for tensile, flexural, and impact mechanical properties
  • Analysed how fibre volume fraction and layup orientation affect overall composite performance
  • Results showed basalt fibre composites offer a lightweight yet strong alternative for automotive, aerospace, and construction applications — with significant sustainability and cost benefits over conventional synthetic fibres

2024 · Robotics Project

Line Tracking Robot

An autonomous line-following robot that detects and follows a line on the floor — designed to demonstrate how control systems, sensors, and automation work in real industrial applications like AGVs and conveyor systems.

  • Designed the mechanical chassis and selected drive components for stable, smooth navigation on the track
  • Integrated an IR sensor array to detect the line path and feed real-time positional data to the controller
  • Programmed control logic to enable accurate path-following with error correction at bends and curves
  • Demonstrated practical relevance to industrial conveyor and Automated Guided Vehicle (AGV) systems
